Just before contact comes, strangely, the heart begins to change first. Nothing has happened yet, but there is a feeling that something is changing.

You haven't moved away from that person, nor have you gotten closer, but there is a moment when the texture of your emotions strangely changes.

Perhaps you were already feeling that change.

---

The person you kept thinking about suddenly has moments when they don't come to mind for a while.

The feeling of wanting to hold on becomes a little looser.

Strangely, that's when contact often begins to come.

So, reunion happens more often because the flow has changed rather than because the other person changed their mind.

---

## The Moment Emotions Begin to Settle, the Flow Changes

The hardest time when waiting for a reunion is when emotions remain strongest.

A time when thoughts of that person filled the day and the heart was shaken by trivial memories.

But from some point, those emotions begin to fade slightly.

It's not that you've completely forgotten, but a state where you're not held as strongly as before.

That change is not simple giving up, but may be a signal that the density of emotions is changing.

---

## Not a Gaze Toward the Other, but the Moment It Returns to Me

While waiting for a reunion, the gaze is mostly directed toward the other person.

Why is there no contact?
What are they thinking right now?

But the moment the flow changes is when that gaze returns to oneself again.

Perhaps at that moment, this thought crossed your mind: What was I feeling in this relationship?

The moment this question begins, the direction of the relationship begins to change slightly.

---

## The Moment Repeated Emotions Stop, the Relationship Also Begins to Move

Emotions after a breakup are mostly repeated.

Longing, regret, expectation, disappointment.

When these emotions continue to circulate, the relationship also stays in the same place.

But at some point, that repetition becomes a little looser.

Only then is there room for the relationship to move in a different direction.

---

## Reunion Starts from a Change in State, Not Timing

Many people view reunion as a matter of time: "When will contact come?"

But there is something more important than time.

It is the state.

When emotions are tilted to one side, the relationship does not move.

But when those emotions begin to settle, the invisible flow begins to move.
